Cinderella

Fairy tale

“Cinderella, you're as lovely as your name Cinderella, you're a sunset in a frame Though you're dressed in rags, you wear an air of queenly grace Anyone can see a throne would be your proper place.”Cinderella is kind to all, especially her mice friends, Jaq and Gus. She has faith that if you keep on believing, your wish will come true. With help from her fairy godmother, she gets a chance to live her dreams.

The most popular version of Cinderella is based on the French story by Charles Perrault, published in 1697, which introduced the fairy godmother, the pumpkin carriage, and the glass slipper.

Another well-known version is the German one by the Brothers Grimm, published in 1812, which featured the stepsisters cutting off parts of their feet to fit the golden slipper and being blinded by pigeons.

Cinderella has inspired many adaptations in film, television, literature, and music, such as Disney's animated and live-action movies, A Cinderella Story with Hilary Duff, and Rodgers and Hammerstein's musical.

Cinderella is also a popular theme in folklore and culture around the world, with variations such as Yeh-Shen in China, Cendrillon in Haiti, and Rhodopis in Egypt.

Cinderella's story is a timeless tale of kindness, courage, and magic that appeals to people of all ages and backgrounds.
